Here's a time line of Gene Kelly's life:

  • 1912 - Eugene CUrran Kelly is born on Augest 23, 1912 in Pittsburgh, PA, the third of five children.
  • 1930 - Starts Gene Kelly Studio of the Dance, with one studio and one in Johnstown.
  • 1933 - Graduates from the University of Pittsburgh with a degree in economics.
  • 1938 - First Broaway job as a dancer in "Leave it to Me." This is also Mary Martins debut.
  • 1941 - Marries Betsy Blair, a dancer Gene met while choreographing Billy Rose's "Diamond Horseshoe."
  • 1942 - Hollywood debut in "For Me and My Gal with Judy Garland.
  • 1944 - Teams with Rita Hayworth in "Cover Girl" which includes a sequence of Kelly dancing with a superimposed image of himself.
  • 1945 - "Anchors Aweigh" earns Kelly a nomination for Best Actor. It is Kelly's first performance with Frank Sinatra, whom he teaches to dance.
  • 1946 - Teams up with Fred Astaire for the "Babbitt and the Bromide" dance swquence in "Ziegfeld Follies."
  • 1949 - "On the Town" earns Gene Kelly and Stanley Donen their directorial stripes.
  • 1951 - "An American in Paris" wins seven Oscars including Best Picture and a special Oscar highlighting Kelly's work in the film.
  • 1952 - Co-directs and stars in "Sinng in the Rain" which has since become one of America's most popular musicals.
  • 1955 - "It's Always Fair Weather" marks the last film co-directed with Donen.
  • 1957 - "Les Girls is Kelly's last starring musical. Kelly divorces Betsy, wife of fifteen years.
  • 1960 - Marries Jannie Coyne, longtime dance assistant and former pupil.
  • 1974 - Hosts "That's Entertainment", a compilation film of MGM Studio's greatest musical moments.
  • 1976 - Shares co-hosting duties of "That's Entertainment, Part 2" with Fred Astaire.
  • 1982 - Recieves the Kennedy Center Honors.
  • 1985 - Awarded Lifetime Achievment Award from the American Film Insitute.
  • 1996 - Dies at hom on February 2 after a series of strokes.
